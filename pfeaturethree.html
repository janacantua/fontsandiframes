HAILEY MARMOLEJO:
VISUALS THAT FEEL LIKE HOME

Photos Courtesy of Hailey Mermolejo

When I first saw Hailey's work, I was driving on Broadway street and captivated by an image of an agave plant that was sprouting.
Butterflies surrounded it and you could see the roots of the plant in the ground. There was wrapped font on the painting that read:
"They tried to bury us, but they didn't know we were seeds."
That message really captivates a lot of Hailey's work. Her art is full of hope, strength and it reminds me of home. Her use of color theory and texture brings so much life to the artwork. And her subject matter is firmly rooted, confident, and encourages the viewer to do the same.
